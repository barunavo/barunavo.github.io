Learning German in Germany

A Journey of Language, Culture, and Determination

Stepping into a foreign country brings with it a unique set of challenges and opportunities. This is the story of how we navigated the journey of learning German while building our careers in Germany.

The Challenge

In October 2017, we arrived in Essen, a city in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. While the professional opportunity was exciting, we quickly realized that daily life would be significantly easier with proficiency in the local language. Most interactions—from grocery shopping to administrative tasks—were conducted in German, making language learning not just beneficial but essential for integration.

This linguistic barrier transformed everyday tasks into challenges. We were determined to overcome this obstacle and truly integrate into our new environment rather than remaining isolated in an expat bubble.

Finding the Right Path

After researching various language learning options, we enrolled at Volkshochschule (VHS) Essen, a well-established institution offering intensive German language courses. The VHS system in Germany provides accessible, structured language education for immigrants and expats, making it an ideal choice for working professionals.

We committed to an intensive course structure that would take us from beginner to B1 level proficiency—a significant milestone that demonstrates independent language use in familiar situations.

Balancing Work and Study

As working professionals in the tech industry, balancing full-time employment with intensive language studies required careful time management and unwavering commitment. Our typical day looked like this:

  • • 07:00 - Start the day and commute to our offices in Dortmund
  • • 08:00-16:15 - Full workday as DevOps/Software Engineers
  • • 16:15-17:15 - Rush hour commute from Dortmund to Essen for classes
  • • 17:30-20:30 - German language classes (3 evenings per week)
  • • 21:00+ - Return home, prepare meals, study, and manage household tasks

This schedule was particularly challenging during winter months when darkness fell early and weather conditions made commuting more difficult. However, we remained focused on our goal of achieving fluency.

The Support System

One of the key factors in our success was the understanding and support from our professional colleagues. They recognized the importance of language integration and were flexible when we needed to leave on time for classes. This workplace support made a significant difference in our ability to maintain this demanding schedule.

Our language instructors at VHS Essen were also instrumental in our success. They brought not just linguistic expertise but also cultural insights that helped us understand the context behind the language. Their dedication to teaching and patience with adult learners from diverse backgrounds created an encouraging learning environment.

The Journey to B1

For eighteen months, we maintained this rigorous schedule, attending classes three times per week while working full-time. The commitment extended beyond classroom hours—we studied during commutes, practiced conversations at home, and immersed ourselves in German media to improve comprehension.

The journey taught us valuable lessons beyond language:

  • ✓ Discipline: Maintaining consistency despite fatigue and competing priorities
  • ✓ Time Management: Optimizing every hour to balance work, study, and personal life
  • ✓ Cultural Integration: Language as a gateway to understanding German society and culture
  • ✓ Resilience: Pushing through challenges and setbacks in the learning process

Achievement and Validation

Our dedication and hard work culminated in successfully passing the B1 German language examination on our first attempt. This achievement was more than just a certification—it represented:

  • ▹ Validation of eighteen months of consistent effort and sacrifice
  • ▹ A milestone in our integration into German society
  • ▹ Enhanced professional opportunities and workplace integration
  • ▹ Greater confidence in navigating daily life independently
  • ▹ Deeper appreciation for German culture and society

Reflections and Advice

Looking back at this experience, several insights stand out for anyone considering a similar journey:

Key Takeaways for Language Learners

  1. Start Early: Begin language learning as soon as possible after arrival
  2. Structured Learning: Formal courses provide accountability and systematic progress
  3. Consistency Over Intensity: Regular practice beats sporadic cramming
  4. Workplace Support: Communicate your language learning goals to employers
  5. Immersion Matters: Supplement classes with real-world practice
  6. Cultural Context: Language and culture are inseparable—learn both
  7. Patience with Yourself: Progress isn't always linear; persistence pays off

Conclusion

Our journey of learning German was about more than acquiring a new language—it was about embracing integration, building connections, and establishing ourselves in a new culture. The experience taught us that with determination, proper support systems, and consistent effort, seemingly insurmountable challenges become achievable milestones.

For anyone embarking on a similar journey of working abroad and learning a new language: the path may be demanding, but the rewards—both professional and personal—make every early morning, late evening class, and weekend study session worthwhile. Language is the key that unlocks not just communication, but true cultural understanding and belonging in your new home.
